Utah Gems

Favorite Courses in Utah

Utah's dramatic landscapes make for some of the most breathtaking golf in the country. These are my go-to rounds.

Thanksgiving Point Golf Club

📍 Lehi, Utah

Designed by Johnny Miller, this 7,728-yard course is one of the longest in the state with stunning mountain views from every tee box.

Wolf Creek Golf Resort

📍 Eden, Utah

Nestled in Ogden Valley near Pineview Reservoir, Wolf Creek offers dramatic elevation changes and postcard-perfect Wasatch scenery.

East Bay Golf Course

📍 Provo, Utah

A local favorite for BYU students and Provo residents alike. Affordable, well-maintained, and framed by the Wasatch Front.

Coral Canyon Golf Course

📍 Washington, Utah

Red rock country golf at its finest. This St. George-area course plays through stunning canyon terrain with desert beauty unlike anywhere else.

Getting Started

Tips for New Golfers

Golf has a steep learning curve, but a few fundamentals make all the difference. Here are the things I wish someone had told me on day one.

01

Master the Grip First

A neutral grip is the foundation of every good swing. Before anything else, spend time getting comfortable with how you hold the club.

02

Short Game = Score

Most strokes happen within 100 yards. Practice chipping and putting far more than driving — your scorecard will thank you.

03

Play Ready Golf

Keep the pace of play moving. Be ready to hit when it's your turn — other golfers on the course will appreciate it.

04

Embrace the Mental Game

Golf is 90% mental. Learn to let bad shots go immediately — every hole is a fresh start. Composure is a skill just like swing mechanics.
